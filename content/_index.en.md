# Yanmar Data-Driven Sustainable Agriculture Course

Shaping the future of sustainable food production through data and technology.

A university–industry joint course established by the Graduate School of Agriculture, Kyoto University, and Yanmar Agri Co., Ltd. We pursue next-generation agriculture that produces food while protecting the environment, through AI, autonomous driving, electrification, and data utilization.

## About the Course

Achieving sustainable food production requires both smart agriculture powered by AI and data, and decarbonized agriculture that emits no greenhouse gases. This course connects Yanmar's agricultural machinery and robotics technologies with the academic expertise of Kyoto University's Graduate School of Agriculture, aiming to solve these challenges and accelerate the advancement of agriculture.

Over five years, from February 2025 to January 2030, we work to establish a data-driven farming system that collects and analyzes field, crop, and soil data in the cloud to enable remote farm management. The research advances through ongoing field demonstrations at sites including Kyoto University's Kizu Farm.

## Research Themes

- **Work systems with autonomous & electric machinery** — Demonstrating integrated farming operations using DX and GX technologies.
- **Use of renewable energy** — Establishing integrated workflows and fixed-point observation with electric machinery.
- **Measurement & analysis of organic fertilizers** — Research into rice-husk biochar, bio-composters, and related approaches.
- **Resource circulation via microorganisms** — Reducing material use and improving productivity with beneficial microbes.
- **Training the next generation** — Developing engineers and researchers for sustainable food production.
