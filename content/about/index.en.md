# About the Course

## Background

Agriculture is an essential industry for society, producing food and energy while conserving the environment. Realizing sustainable food production calls for both smart agriculture powered by AI and data, and decarbonized agriculture that emits no greenhouse gases.

This course connects Yanmar's agricultural machinery and robotics technologies with the academic expertise cultivated by Kyoto University's Graduate School of Agriculture, with the aim of solving these challenges and accelerating the advancement of agriculture.

## Purpose

Beyond further advancing autonomous-driving and electrification technologies, the course aims to establish a data-driven farming system that collects field, crop, and soil data in the cloud and enables remote farm management. In parallel, through lectures, guidance on research projects, and cultivation and operation analyses that involve students and research students, the course develops the engineers and researchers who will lead the next generation of sustainable food production. Field demonstrations are carried out at sites including Kyoto University's Kizu Farm.
